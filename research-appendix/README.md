# Research Appendix — Consciousness & Unexplained Phenomena

**This is *not* part of *Consciousness for Eternity* (CoE).** It is a companion set of
research tools and evidence maps, kept deliberately **separate** from the canonical
manuscript. Nothing here is imported into the CoE `.md`, and the CoE document does not
depend on anything here.

## Why it's separate

Per CoE §6.6, a single retrospective personal experience (`n=1`) is `[Hypo]` **by
definition** — legitimate as a *question-raiser*, never as evidence. This appendix keeps
that firewall intact: it works only with **general, published research**. It contains **no
personal or family information**. The tools treat lived experience as a *question* (✅),
not an *answer* (❌) — answers come only from the `[Fact]` wall.

## Epistemic discipline

Same house style as CoE: every claim is tagged — `[Fact]` (measured / peer-reviewed),
`[Fact-eq]` (derived, arithmetic shown), `[Hypo]` (assumption). Independent researcher
convergence is cited **for priority, not confirmation** — it raises robustness, not truth.

## The two-layer rule (the reusable tool)

Every "downloaded from nature / unlocked psychic access" claim is sorted by:

- **① Pattern** — really present in nature, catchable without being taught; brain
  suppression/injury *can* disinhibit it (shape, rhythm, symmetry, rough quantity). Mundane.
- **② Meaning / language / knowledge** — human convention, *not* in nature, must be
  learned, cannot be downloaded (vocabulary, grammar, math symbols, theorems).

A "received it from nature" claim that delivers only ① is ordinary internal disinhibition.
It would count as real evidence only if it ever delivered ② (learned-only knowledge) — which
has never once happened. Grounded in ANS vs symbolic number / Pirahã (Gordon 2004; Frank et
al. 2008), statistical learning (Saffran et al. 1996), arbitrariness of the sign (Saussure),
the symbol grounding problem (Harnad 1990), and savant disinhibition (Snyder 2009).
