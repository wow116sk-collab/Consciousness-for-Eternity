A · Survival of self

CONTESTEDReincarnation / past-life memory (Stevenson/Tucker)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)James Leininger (Tucker, Explore 2016): a 2-year-old made statements/behaviors matched to WWII pilot James M. Huston Jr. (Corsair pilot, USS Natoma Bay, shot down off Chichi-Jima 1945). Broader corpus: Stevenson's DOPS catalogued ~2,500-3,000 'cases of the reincarnation type,' a subset with claimed birthmark/birth-defect correspondence to the previous person's fatal wounds (Reincarnation and Biology, 1997).

ตัวเลข~2,500-3,000 catalogued cases over ~40 yrs; Leininger case rests almost entirely on 2 parental witnesses; ~200 birthmark cases claimed with medical-record match in a fraction.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Retrospective and uncontrolled: nearly all evidentiary weight is unrecorded parental testimony gathered after the child was identified with a specific deceased person, with documented exposure to ordinary information sources (books, media, family talk) and no prospective, pre-identification recording. Base rates of childhood fantasy/confabulation uncontrolled. Sudduth's and the JSE re-examination show the strongest Leininger 'hits' were logged after the identification was made.

Tucker JB (2016) 'The Case of James Leininger,' Explore 12(3):200-207 · Stevenson I (1997) Reincarnation and Biology, Praeger · critical re-examination, Journal of Scientific Exploration (2021); Sudduth M (2016) critique of the Leininger case
CONTESTEDNear-death experience (AWARE / AWARE-II)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Parnia's prospective AWARE (2014) and AWARE-II (2023) multi-center studies of cardiac arrest with hidden visual/auditory targets and (AWARE-II) intra-arrest EEG. AWARE-II reported EEG activity (gamma/delta/theta spikes) consistent with a return toward normal patterns up to 35-60 min into CPR despite ischemia, plus recalled 'transcendent' experiences.

ตัวเลขAWARE 2014: 2,060 arrests, 330 survivors, 140 interviewed, ~9% NDE, 2% explicit awareness, 1 verifiable auditory case; 0 hidden visual targets identified. AWARE-II 2023: 567 enrolled, ~53 survivors interviewed (28 centers), ~11 reported experiences, 0/─ identified the image, 1 identified the auditory stimulus.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)The prospective, fraud-proof test (hidden targets) failed: across both studies not a single patient identified the concealed visual image, so no verified veridical out-of-brain perception was demonstrated. EEG spikes are a mundane physiological finding compatible with residual brain activity, not evidence of consciousness surviving zero brain function. Recalled experiences remain retrospective self-report.

Parnia S et al. (2014) 'AWARE—AWAreness during REsuscitation,' Resuscitation 85(12):1799-1805 · Parnia S et al. (2023) 'AWAreness during REsuscitation-II,' Resuscitation 191:109903
CONTESTEDMediumship / after-death communication

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Beischel & Schwartz triple-blind protocol (Explore 2007): mediums, sitters, raters, and experimenter all blinded; readings scored against target vs. decoy discarnate. Sitters selected their own reading above chance in a forced-choice test; replicated/extended by Windbridge (Beischel 2015; triple-blind 2021).

ตัวเลข2007: 8 mediums, significant target>decoy accuracy. 2021 triple-blind: item-accuracy target vs decoy n=31, P=.002, d=0.75; 20 mediums, 86 readings. Effects moderate, single lab.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)No independent replication outside the Windbridge group; rater-scoring subjectivity and rating-bias, plus incomplete control of cold-reading/generic-statement (Barnum) effects and possible sensory leakage in phone protocols. Small single-source literature vulnerable to selective reporting; sincerity/subjective conviction is not accuracy.

Beischel J & Schwartz GE (2007) 'Anomalous Information Reception by Research Mediums...Triple-Blind Protocol,' Explore 3(1):23-27 · Beischel J et al. (2015) replication, Explore 11(2):136-142 · Rock AJ, Beischel J et al. (2021) triple-blind study, Explore
ESTABLISHEDTerminal lucidity

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Nahm, Greyson, Kelly & Haraldsson (2012) retrospective review/case collection of unexpected pre-death mental clarity in end-stage dementia; Batthyány & Greyson (2021) caregiver survey documenting spontaneous return of coherent communication days-to-minutes before death. Mashour et al. (2019, Alzheimer's & Dementia) legitimized 'paradoxical lucidity' as a real neurobiological research target.

ตัวเลขNahm 2012: 83 retrospective cases, ~43% died within a day, ~84% within a week of the episode. Batthyány collection: 38 episodes, durations from <10 min (3%) to several days (5%); caregiver-report prevalence high but retrospective.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Interpretively neutral: the phenomenon is real but is fully compatible with mundane neurobiology (transient network re-activation, reduced inflammation/edema); it is not evidence for survival of self. Evidence base is retrospective anecdote/survey with no prospective physiological documentation and recall/selection bias.

Nahm M, Greyson B, Kelly EW, Haraldsson E (2012) 'Terminal lucidity: A review and case collection,' Archives of Gerontology and Geriatrics 55(1):138-142 · Batthyány A & Greyson B (2021) 'Spontaneous remission of dementia before death,' Psychology of Consciousness 8(1) · Mashour GA et al. (2019) 'Paradoxical lucidity,' Alzheimer's & Dementia 15(8):1107-1114
CONTESTEDXenoglossy

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Stevenson's two 'responsive xenoglossy' cases (Unlearned Language, 1984): under hypnosis 'Jensen' (Swedish) and 'Gretchen' (German) allegedly held two-way conversations in a language the subject had not learned; Stevenson documented no evidence of deliberate fraud.

ตัวเลข2 principal cases; 'Gretchen' German vocabulary judged 'minute,' pronunciation 'spotty'; responses largely 1-2 word replies or echoed questions; no standardized word-list/translation test administered.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Linguist Sarah Thomason's reanalysis: the subjects could not converse fluently, and Stevenson's 'responsive' criterion permitted yes/no guessing and generous interpretation of vague replies; subjects had prior incidental exposure to the language. Evidence too weak to establish genuine unlearned fluency; not true responsive xenoglossy.

Stevenson I (1984) Unlearned Language: New Studies in Xenoglossy, Univ. Press of Virginia · Thomason SG (1987) 'Past Tongues Remembered?' Skeptical Inquirer 11:367-375 · Thomason SG (1996) 'Xenoglossy,' in The Encyclopedia of the Paranormal (Stein, ed.)
CONTESTEDCrisis apparitions

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)SPR Census of Hallucinations (Sidgwick et al., 1894): large systematic survey of waking hallucinations; 'death-coincidence' apparitions (percipient sees a person at/near that person's unexpected death) occurred far above chance. Precursor corpus: Gurney, Myers & Podmore, Phantasms of the Living (1886).

ตัวเลข~17,000 respondents; 1,684 reported a hallucination; 30 well-attested death-coincidence cases, computed at ~440x the chance expectation (using a 12-hour window around death).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Retrospective self-report with uncontrolled memory distortion and post-hoc date-matching; the arbitrary coincidence window and reliance on recalled timing inflate the ratio, and non-coincident hallucinations are underreported (denominator bias). No prospective registration of experience-before-notification. Base-rate/selection artifacts plausibly account for the excess.

Sidgwick H et al. (1894) 'Report on the Census of Hallucinations,' Proceedings of the SPR 10:25-422 · Gurney E, Myers FWH, Podmore F (1886) Phantasms of the Living · West DJ (1948) critical analysis of the Census (Proceedings of the SPR)
CONTESTEDVeridical NDE/OBE (Pam Reynolds, Maria's shoe)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Pam Reynolds (Sabom, Light and Death, 1998): during a hypothermic cardiac-standstill aneurysm operation she later reported specific surgical details (bone-saw description, overheard remark) during a period argued to be flat-EEG. Maria's shoe (Clark, 1984): a resuscitated CCU patient reportedly saw, out-of-body, a tennis shoe on a hospital ledge.

ตัวเลขReynolds: 1 case, ~4°C 'standstill' phase; disputed whether the recalled events coincided with EEG silence vs. general anesthesia hours earlier. Maria: 1 case, 1977, first documented ~7 yrs later.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Both are single retrospective anecdotes with mundane explanations. Reynolds' verifiable perceptions occurred during anesthesia (auditory 'anesthesia awareness'), not during the flat-EEG standstill (Woerlee); molded ear-speakers still transmit bone-conducted sound. For Maria, Ebbern, Mulligan & Beyerstein (1996) showed the shoe was easily visible/ inferable from inside the ward and the story was uncorroborated and inflated over 7 years. Prospective hidden-target NDE studies (AWARE) yielded zero confirmed veridical OBE perceptions.

Sabom M (1998) Light and Death, Zondervan · Ebbern H, Mulligan S, Beyerstein BL (1996) 'Maria's Near-Death Experience: Waiting for the Other Shoe to Drop,' Skeptical Inquirer 20(4):27-33 · Woerlee GM (2005) 'Could Pam Reynolds Hear?' Journal of Near-Death Studies
CONTESTEDPast-life regression (hypnosis, Bridey Murphy)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The Search for Bridey Murphy (Bernstein, 1956): hypnotized Virginia Tighe produced a detailed, apparently accurate 19th-century Irish 'Bridey Murphy' persona with period specifics—historically the marquee regression case cited as veridical.

ตัวเลข1 case, 6 sessions (1952-53); several 'Irish' details later traced to Tighe's Chicago childhood; Spanos (1991) experimental series: hypnotic past-life identities were richer when experimenters cued expectations (n across studies, all suggestion-driven).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Cryptomnesia + suggestion: the veridical details were traceable to Tighe's ordinary childhood memories (a neighbor named Bridie Murphy Corkell; part-Irish upbringing), not to a past life. Controlled experiments (Spanos) show regression 'memories' are confabulations shaped by hypnotist cues and subject expectation, indistinguishable from—often more vivid than—real memories. No case has produced independently unlearnable veridical information.

Bernstein M (1956) The Search for Bridey Murphy, Doubleday · Spanos NP et al. (1991) 'Secondary Identity Enactments During Hypnotic Past-Life Regression,' Journal of Personality and Social Psychology 61(2):308-320 · Baker RA (1982) study of hypnotic past-life confabulation, International Journal of Clinical and Experimental Hypnosis

B · Anomalous cognition (info in / psi)

CONTESTEDTelepathy / Ganzfeld

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Storm, Tressoldi & Di Risio (2010, Psychological Bulletin) meta-analysis of 29 ganzfeld studies (1997-2008). Receiver in sensory homogenization (halved ping-pong balls, red light, white noise) tries to pick the 1-of-4 target image a distant sender viewed. The modern methodological anchor is Bem & Honorton (1994) autoganzfeld at PRL, computer-automated to close Hyman's earlier flaw list, also ~32%.

ตัวเลขStorm 2010: 1,498 trials, 483 hits = 32.2% vs 25% chance, p < .001; selected/believer participants scored higher. Bem & Honorton 1994: ~32% across 354 trials. Countervailing: Milton & Wiseman (1999, Psych Bulletin) meta-analysis of 30 studies (1987-1997) found NO significant effect (Stouffer Z ~0).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Replication instability: the database flips between significant (Bem-Honorton, Storm) and null (Milton-Wiseman) depending on which trials are pooled. Hyman (2010) showed Storm's set mixes heterogeneous protocols and that outcome tracks study quality/selection. A small aggregate effect surviving only in selective meta-analysis, with no pre-registered high-powered lab result others reproduce on demand, is the signature of QRPs plus optional stopping, not a stable signal.

Storm, Tressoldi & Di Risio (2010, Psychological Bulletin 136:471-485) · Milton & Wiseman (1999, Psychological Bulletin 125:387-391) · Hyman (2010, Psychological Bulletin 136:486-490)
CONTESTEDPrecognition (Bem)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Bem (2011, Journal of Personality and Social Psychology), 'Feeling the Future': 9 experiments time-reversing standard cognitive paradigms (e.g., erotic-stimulus precognitive detection, retroactive priming, retroactive facilitation of recall). 8 of 9 reached significance. Follow-up: Bem, Tressoldi, Rabeyron & Duggan (2015) meta-analysis of 90 experiments claiming replication.

ตัวเลขBem 2011: mean effect size d = 0.22 across 9 studies, ~1,000 participants, most p < .05. 2015 meta-analysis: 90 experiments, combined effect d ~0.09, p ~1.2e-10. Failed replications: Ritchie, Wiseman & French (2012, PLOS ONE) 3 pre-registered attempts, combined n=150, combined p=.83 (one-tailed, null); Galak, LeBoeuf, Nelson & Simmons (2012, JPSP) 7 experiments, >3,000 participants, effect ~0.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Prospective, high-powered, pre-registered independent replications (Galak n>3,000; Ritchie-Wiseman-French) return flat null. Wagenmakers et al. (2011) showed Bem's own data collapse under default Bayesian tests and exhibit p-hacking / flexible analysis. Bem's paper became the textbook case that triggered psychology's replication crisis: it passed peer review precisely because the field's standard methods permit false positives. Retrospective meta-analytic 'success' cannot rescue a claim that fails prospectively.

Bem (2011, J. Personality & Social Psychology 100:407-425) · Ritchie, Wiseman & French (2012, PLOS ONE 7:e33423) · Galak, LeBoeuf, Nelson & Simmons (2012, J. Personality & Social Psychology 103:933-948)
CONTESTEDRemote viewing / Stargate

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The strongest FOR reading is Jessica Utts's statistical evaluation within the 1995 CIA/American Institutes for Research (AIR) review of the ~20-year, ~$20M government program (SRI/SAIC lab studies plus operational tasking). Utts concluded a statistically reliable lab effect existed and was replicated across labs.

ตัวเลขUtts: lab effect sizes ~0.2 (some 'star' subjects 5-15% above chance); she argued replication across SRI and SAIC. Program cost ~$20M over ~1972-1995. AIR operational assessment: essentially zero actionable intelligence produced.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Co-reviewer Ray Hyman (same 1995 report) identified sensory-cueing and judging/feedback leakage, non-independent replication, and lack of a mechanism; the CIA concluded remote viewing was never operationally useful and terminated the program. Even granting a small lab effect, 'operationally useless' plus documented protocol leakage (targets, judging cues) means the effect is best explained by methodological artifact, not anomalous cognition. No blinded, leak-proof, pre-registered replication has been demonstrated since.

Utts (1996, J. Scientific Exploration 10:3-30, AIR report component) · Hyman (1996, J. Scientific Exploration 10:31-58) · Mumford, Rose & Goslin / AIR (1995, 'An Evaluation of Remote Viewing', CIA)
ESTABLISHEDDivination / astrology

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)There is no credible positive result under controlled conditions; the 'best' cited FOR is Ertel's (2009, J. Scientific Exploration) reanalysis of Carlson's data claiming astrologers matched profiles above chance when the full sample is combined. The definitive test itself, Shawn Carlson (1985, Nature), was double-blind and found no effect.

ตัวเลขCarlson 1985: >100 subjects, 28 astrologers rating California Psychological Inventory profiles against natal charts; astrologers performed at chance (predicted vs actual matching not significantly different; astrologers' own confidence uncorrelated with accuracy). Ertel 2009 reanalysis claimed a small effect (~p<.05) on a subset. Also Dean & Kelly (2003) review of 40+ studies incl. time-twin data: null.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Not-empirical / mundane mechanism plus failed replication. Every well-controlled, blinded test returns chance performance; documented positive impressions are explained by the Barnum-Forer effect (accepting vague generic descriptions), cold reading, and confirmation bias. Ertel's reanalysis is a retrospective re-slicing of a null dataset and is itself contested. Astrology also has no plausible physical mechanism and fails on interpretive-neutrality (charts are read post hoc).

Carlson (1985, Nature 318:419-425) · Dean & Kelly (2003, J. Consciousness Studies 10:175-198) · Ertel (2009, J. Scientific Exploration 23:125-137)
CONTESTEDPresentiment / predictive anticipatory activity

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Mossbridge, Tressoldi & Utts (2012, Frontiers in Psychology) meta-analysis of 'predictive physiological anticipatory activity' (PAA): physiological measures (skin conductance, heart rate, pupil, EEG) differ in the seconds BEFORE a randomly-selected emotional vs neutral/calm stimulus is presented.

ตัวเลขMossbridge 2012: 26 studies from 7 labs, overall effect size ~0.21 (95% CI 0.15-0.27), p < 2.7e-12; claimed fail-safe N ~87. Updated Duggan & Tressoldi (2018) meta-analysis: effect ~0.28 (95% CI 0.18-0.38).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mundane statistical artifact. Schwarzkopf (2014, Frontiers) and Wagenmakers-style critics show the effect is fully explainable by expectation/anticipation bias, non-random stimulus sequences, physiological baseline-drift and multiple-comparison / analysis-flexibility artifacts rather than backward causation. Effect appears in unblinded, non-pre-registered small-N designs and is not established by high-powered pre-registered independent replication; retrospective meta-analysis of heterogeneous physiological measures cannot rule out these mundane confounds.

Mossbridge, Tressoldi & Utts (2012, Frontiers in Psychology 3:390) · Schwarzkopf (2014, Frontiers in Human Neuroscience 8:332) · Duggan & Tressoldi (2018, F1000Research / PMC6124390)

C · Mind on matter (PK)

CONTESTEDMicro-PK / RNG (PEAR)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The cumulative PEAR laboratory database (Robert Jahn & Brenda Dunne, Princeton, 1979–2007): ~1.5 billion+ REG trials in which volunteers tried to bias electronic random-number-generator output toward HI/LO. Cumulatively PEAR reported a directional shift consistent with intention, and the broader field was anchored by Radin & Nelson's 1989 meta-analysis of 832 RNG studies reporting odds against chance beyond a trillion to one.

ตัวเลขPEAR ~1.5B+ trials, hit rate ~50.02% vs 50% chance (effect size ~1e-4). Radin & Nelson 1989: 832 studies, odds >1e12:1. Bosch et al. 2006: 380 studies, tiny significant pooled effect but sample-size-dependent and heterogeneous. Jahn et al. 2000 replication: positive but non-significant at all 3 labs (primary measure failed).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Publication-bias / small-study artifact plus replication failure. Bosch, Steinkamp & Boller's 2006 meta-analysis (380 studies) found the effect size strongly and inversely tied to sample size with extreme heterogeneity and funnel asymmetry — the signature of selective reporting; the largest, most controlled datasets converge on the null. The formal three-lab replication (PEAR/Giessen/Freiburg, Jahn et al. 2000) failed to reproduce the original result, and a 227-participant/2M-trial replication also failed. Effect, if real, is ~1 part in 10,000 — indistinguishable from residual method bias.

Bosch, Steinkamp & Boller (2006), Psychological Bulletin 132(4):497–523 · Jahn, Mischo, Vaitl et al. (2000), Journal of Scientific Exploration 14(4) — three-lab replication · Radin & Nelson (1989), Foundations of Physics 19:1499–1514
CONTESTEDPoltergeist / RSPK

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The Rosenheim case (Bavaria, 1967–68), investigated by parapsychologist Hans Bender with Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics physicists Friedbert Karger and Gerhard Zicha, who logged anomalous electrical events (deflected recording instruments, telephone/lighting disturbances) said to cluster around 19-year-old secretary Annemarie Schaberl and to stop when she left. Presented as real-time instrumented monitoring of RSPK.

ตัวเลขRosenheim 1967–68; focus person aged 19. Tina Resch/Columbus 1984, focus person aged 14, caught on video faking (event later ascribed to poltergeist); Randi photo analysis of Fred Shannon/Columbus Dispatch images. No RSPK effect ever reproduced under blinded, instrumented control.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)No case survives controlled observation without fraud or mundane cause. At Rosenheim the phone anomalies were also consistent with equipment/wiring faults and were never captured as genuine PK under blinded control; effects tracked one person who was never continuously watched. The best-documented modern case, the Columbus/Tina Resch poltergeist (1984), was directly caught: a TV crew's inadvertently-running camera filmed Resch pulling over a lamp herself, and Randi's analysis showed the famous 'flying phone' photos were thrown objects. Cases are retrospective, uncontrolled, and centered on an unmonitored 'focus' agent — the classic profile of trickery/misperception.

Bender (1969), on the Rosenheim case, Proceedings of the Parapsychological Association 5 · Roll & Storey (2004), Unleashed: Of Poltergeists and Murder — The Curious Story of Tina Resch · Nickell (2012/Skeptical Inquirer), 'Demons and Saviors: Revisiting the Columbus Poltergeist Case'
CONTESTEDFaith healing / intercessory prayer

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The claimed-positive anchor is Byrd (1988, Southern Medical Journal), a 393-patient coronary-care-unit RCT reporting fewer complications in the prayed-for group, echoed by Harris et al. (1999, Archives of Internal Medicine, n=990) on a weighted outcome score. These are the most-cited RCTs offered for a real effect of distant intercessory prayer.

ตัวเลขSTEP 2006: n=1,802; complications 52% (prayed, uncertain) vs 51% (not prayed, uncertain); 59% in the 'certain of prayer' arm (worse, p=0.025 vs uncertain). Cochrane 2009: 10 trials, ~7,646 patients, no significant benefit. Byrd 1988: n=393; Harris 1999: n=990 (weighted composite score only).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)The largest, most rigorous prospective trial nulls it and the Byrd/Harris positives rest on post hoc / multiply-tested outcome scores. STEP (Benson et al. 2006, American Heart Journal), the biggest RCT (1,802 CABG patients, 3 arms, standardized prayer), found no effect: 52% vs 51% complications in the blinded arms — and patients told they were being prayed for did slightly worse (59%). The Cochrane systematic review (Roberts et al. 2009, 10 studies, ~7,646 patients) found no significant effect on death, clinical state, or readmission. Prospective + blinded beats the smaller retrospective-scoring positives; sincerity of prayer is not evidence of causal efficacy.

Benson, Dusek, Sherwood et al. (2006), American Heart Journal 151(4):934–942 (STEP) · Roberts, Ahmed, Hall & Davison (2009), Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, CD000368 · Byrd (1988), Southern Medical Journal 81(7):826–829

D · Anomalous experience (mundane mechanism)

ESTABLISHEDOut-of-body experience (TPJ)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Blanke et al. (2002, Nature) reproducibly evoked OBEs by focal electrical stimulation of the right temporoparietal junction / angular gyrus in an epilepsy patient during pre-surgical mapping; escalating current produced graded body-schema distortions then full disembodied self-location and autoscopy. This is the strongest case FOR OBE as a real, evocable neural state — but it locates the cause inside the brain, not outside it. The claimed-anomalous reading (consciousness literally leaving the body and veridically perceiving remote targets) has its own best test in the AWARE / AWARE-II prospective cardiac-arrest studies, which planted hidden visual targets in resuscitation rooms.

ตัวเลขBlanke 2002: single patient, stimulation-locked OBEs; effect replicated across later TPJ stimulation/lesion and vestibular-conflict studies. AWARE (Parnia et al. 2014, Resuscitation): 2060 cardiac arrests, 4 UK/US/Austria hospitals, ~330 hidden targets; 0 of the verifiable OBE reports involved a patient who saw a planted target — target-hit rate 0%.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mundane mechanism + failed veridicality test. The experience is fully explained by multisensory (vestibular-visual-proprioceptive) disintegration at the TPJ and is evocable on demand by stimulation; the extraordinary claim (accurate out-of-body perception) has never been demonstrated under prospective controls — AWARE returned a 0% hit rate on planted targets.

Blanke, Ortigue, Landis & Seeck (2002) Nature 419:269 — 'Stimulating illusory own-body perceptions' · Blanke & Arzy (2005) The Neuroscientist 11(1):16-24 — 'The out-of-body experience: disturbed self-processing at the temporo-parietal junction' · Parnia et al. (2014) Resuscitation 85(12):1799-1805 — 'AWARE—AWAreness during REsuscitation'
ESTABLISHEDSleep paralysis

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Sleep paralysis is the best-characterized 'anomalous experience' here: it is a physiologically documented dissociated state in which REM-sleep muscle atonia persists into wakefulness, with EEG/EMG polysomnographic confirmation, and it reliably generates the classic 'intruder / incubus / chest-pressure / floating' hallucinations that folk traditions read as demons, witches, or alien abductors. Cross-cultural work (Hufford; the Cambodian 'khmaoch sangkat', Newfoundland 'Old Hag') shows the same sensory template producing culturally-variable supernatural interpretations — evidence the percept is endogenous, not veridical contact.

ตัวเลขSharpless & Barber (2011) Sleep Med Rev, systematic review of 35 studies, N=36,533: lifetime prevalence 7.6% general population, 28.3% students, 31.9% psychiatric patients (34.6% in panic disorder). Higher rates in minority vs Caucasian samples. Later meta-analyses corroborate ~8% general-population lifetime rate.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Fully explained mundane mechanism (REM atonia + hypnopompic/hypnagogic hallucination). Sincerity and vividness are not accuracy: identical physiology yields incompatible supernatural interpretations across cultures, which is exactly the signature of an internally generated percept, not an external agent.

Sharpless & Barber (2011) Sleep Medicine Reviews 15(5):311-315 — 'Lifetime prevalence rates of sleep paralysis: a systematic review' · Cheyne, Rueffer & Newby-Clark (1999) Consciousness and Cognition 8(3):319-337 — 'Hypnagogic and hypnopompic hallucinations during sleep paralysis' · Hufford (1982) 'The Terror That Comes in the Night', Univ. of Pennsylvania Press
CONTESTEDPsychedelic / mystical experience

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Griffiths et al. (2006, Psychopharmacology) — a double-blind study (psilocybin vs methylphenidate active comparator, crossover) in 36 hallucinogen-naive volunteers — showed high-dose psilocybin reliably occasions 'complete' mystical-type experiences that participants rated, at 14-month follow-up (Griffiths 2008), among the most personally and spiritually significant events of their lives. The 2011 dose-effect study (Griffiths, 18 volunteers, 0/5/10/20/30 mg/70kg) established a clean dose-response. This is robust evidence FOR the reality and durability of the experience — not for its metaphysical content.

ตัวเลขGriffiths 2006: N=36; ~61-67% met criteria for a 'complete' mystical experience at high dose; at 14 months ~58-67% rated it among the 5 most meaningful experiences of their life. MacLean 2011 (n=52 pooled): measurable, sustained increase in trait Openness — one of very few documented adult personality changes from a single intervention.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Interpretive-neutrality / not-empirical for the ontological claim. The occurrence, intensity and lasting psychological effects are established, but 'contact with ultimate reality / God / a noetic truth' is a subjective interpretation of a 5-HT2A-agonist brain state; the durable-meaning finding measures believed significance, not truth of the metaphysics. Placebo/active-comparator blinding is also compromised because effects are obvious to subjects.

Griffiths, Richards, McCann & Jesse (2006) Psychopharmacology 187(3):268-283 · Griffiths et al. (2011) Psychopharmacology 218(4):649-665 — dose-effect study · MacLean, Johnson & Griffiths (2011) J Psychopharmacology 25(11):1453-1461 — increases in Openness
ESTABLISHEDDeja vu

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The mundane account is now experimentally supported. Cleary and colleagues used virtual-reality scenes sharing spatial layout with earlier-studied (but unrecalled) scenes to induce déjà vu on demand in the lab, and further showed the associated 'feeling of prediction / prescience' is an illusory metacognitive byproduct of familiarity, not actual foresight — participants who reported feeling they could predict the next turn performed at chance. Urquhart/O'Connor fMRI work (2018) supports a memory-conflict / familiarity-monitoring account (perirhinal familiarity signal vs hippocampal/frontal novelty check).

ตัวเลขCleary et al. (2012, 2018-2021) VR studies: scenes with matched spatial layout produced significantly higher familiarity ratings and more déjà vu reports under recall failure; predicted-turn accuracy at chance (~50%) despite strong subjective 'prescience'. fMRI (Urquhart et al. 2018) implicates frontal/decision regions, not medial-temporal 'false memory' hyperactivity.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mundane mechanism + base-rate/retrospective. The precognitive ('I knew what would happen next') reading fails a direct prospective test — predictions run at chance. Déjà vu is a familiarity-monitoring glitch; the sense of prophecy is a post-hoc feeling, not accurate prediction.

Cleary et al. (2012) Consciousness and Cognition 21(2):969-975 — VR spatial-layout induction of déjà vu · Cleary & Claxton (2018) Psychological Science 29(4):635-644 — déjà vu does not enable prediction of the future · Urquhart, Sivakumaran, Macfarlane & O'Connor (2018) Cortex 108:263-274 — fMRI, memory-conflict account
CONTESTEDGhost / haunting experiences

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The best FOR-the-environmental-cause evidence is Wiseman et al.'s Hampton Court / Edinburgh Vaults fieldwork (2003), where reported 'presence' hotspots correlated with local physical features (magnetic-field variance, lighting, draughts, ceiling height) — suggesting some experiences track real environmental cues rather than pure imagination. Persinger's earlier work claimed weak transcranial magnetic fields evoke 'sensed presence'.

ตัวเลขWiseman et al. (2003) Hampton Court: N=678 visitors; reported experiences clustered non-randomly in areas of higher magnetic-field variance and specific physical features. The 'Haunt' project (French, Haque, Bunton-Stasyshyn & Davis, 2009, Cortex): N=79 in a purpose-built chamber; 79.7% reported feeling dizzy/odd, but crucially EMF and infrasound conditions produced NO effect above sham — suggestibility was the only significant predictor.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)The physical-cause version FAILS a controlled test and the anomalous version fails too. The Haunt project — the direct experimental manipulation of EMF/infrasound — found no EMF or infrasound effect; only suggestibility/expectation predicted experiences. Persinger's 'God helmet' failed independent replication (Granqvist et al. 2005, Neurosci Lett), where suggestibility, not the field, drove reports. Base-rate + suggestion, not spirits.

Wiseman, Watt, Stevens, Greening & O'Keeffe (2003) British Journal of Psychology 94(2):195-211 — Hampton Court / Edinburgh Vaults · French, Haque, Bunton-Stasyshyn & Davis (2009) Cortex 45(5):619-629 — the 'Haunt' project · Granqvist et al. (2005) Neuroscience Letters 379(1):1-6 — failed replication of Persinger 'sensed presence'
HypoDMT entity encounters

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Davis, Clifton, Weaver, Hurwitz, Johnson & Griffiths (2020, J Psychopharmacology) — a large online survey (Johns Hopkins) documenting the phenomenology of inhaled-DMT 'entity' encounters: highly consistent reports of autonomous, sentient, communicating beings, often experienced as 'more real than reality,' with enduring attitudinal effects. Rick Strassman's earlier controlled DMT-administration study (1990s, University of New Mexico, ~60 volunteers) produced comparable spontaneous entity reports under IV DMT. Strong evidence the experience-type is real, reproducible and pharmacologically triggered.

ตัวเลขDavis 2020: N=2561 respondents describing a single memorable DMT entity encounter. 69% derived a message/insight; a priori atheists dropped from 28% (before) to 10% (after) and 'no doubt entity was conscious/intelligent' endorsed by a large majority. Shift toward belief in a higher power 36%->58%. Strassman (Clinical Pharmacology 1994/'DMT: The Spirit Molecule' 2001): ~50% of high-dose IV sessions (0.4 mg/kg) yielded entity contact.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Not-empirical for the autonomy claim + retrospective self-report. The survey establishes that the percept is consistent and drug-locked; it cannot show the entities exist independently of the 5-HT2A-driven brain state. Reports are retrospective, self-selected, and the 'more real than real' conviction is a known feature of the neurochemistry, not evidence of an external ontology. Convergence with alien-abduction/NDE templates argues for shared endogenous generation.

Davis, Clifton, Weaver, Hurwitz, Johnson & Griffiths (2020) Journal of Psychopharmacology 34(9):1008-1020 · Strassman, Qualls, Uhlenhuth & Kellner (1994) Archives of General Psychiatry 51(2):98-108 — dose-response IV DMT · Strassman (2001) 'DMT: The Spirit Molecule', Park Street Press
CONTESTEDEVP (electronic voice phenomena)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)There is no credible study supporting the paranormal reading; the strongest EMPIRICAL work explains EVP as auditory pareidolia. Nees & Phillips (2015, Applied Cognitive Psychology) showed a 'paranormal' contextual prime made listeners perceive significantly more voices in both genuine EVP clips and degraded speech/noise — and, decisively, listeners showed no agreement on what any given EVP 'said,' the signature of noise-driven pattern completion rather than a real signal. This subsumes the historical verbal-transformation-effect literature.

ตัวเลขNees & Phillips (2015): primed participants reported significantly more voice detections in ambiguous/noise stimuli than unprimed controls; near-zero inter-rater agreement on EVP content. Consistent with earlier findings that a single noise sample yields different 'words' per listener. Related priming/pareidolia work (e.g., Escola-Gascon; sensory-processing-sensitivity studies) reproduces the prime effect.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mundane mechanism + interpretive non-reproducibility. EVP is auditory pareidolia driven by expectation: the 'message' is not reproducible across listeners, there is no controlled protocol yielding above-chance, agreed-upon content, and no mechanism distinguishes claimed voices from random acoustic noise. Fails independent replication and inter-rater agreement — not an empirical phenomenon.

Nees & Phillips (2015) Applied Cognitive Psychology 29(1):129-134 — 'Auditory Pareidolia: contextual priming...' · Warren (1968) Psychological Bulletin 70(4):261-270 — verbal transformation effect (baseline mechanism) · Barušs (2001) Journal of Scientific Exploration 15(3):355-367 — failed systematic EVP replication

E · Brain-mind (established neuroscience)

FactBrain lesions -> deficits

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Patient H.M. (Henry Molaison): after Scoville's 1953 bilateral medial-temporal-lobe resection (hippocampus, amygdala, entorhinal cortex) for intractable epilepsy, he developed dense permanent anterograde amnesia with preserved IQ, perception, language, and procedural learning - a lesion-specific dissociation documented prospectively for ~55 years. Reinforced by Broca's 1861 autopsy case 'Tan' (left inferior frontal lesion -> expressive aphasia) and Damasio et al. 1994 CT reconstruction of Phineas Gage's ventromedial-prefrontal damage -> decision/personality deficits. Most reproducible evidence that specific mental functions depend causally on specific neural tissue.

ตัวเลขH.M. followed ~55 yr (1953 surgery -> 2008 death); ~5 cm bilateral medial temporal resection; digit span ~6 preserved yet zero new declarative memory. Broca 1861 n=1, autopsy-confirmed left F3. Lesion effects are categorical (function abolished vs intact), not statistical.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Interpretive-neutrality: lesion->deficit proves the brain is NECESSARY for and modulates mind, but is equally consistent with brain-as-instrument/transceiver as with brain-generates-mind. It establishes dependence, not identity - damaging a radio distorts the broadcast without the radio creating it.

Scoville & Milner 1957, J Neurol Neurosurg Psychiatry · Broca 1861, Bulletin de la Societe Anatomique de Paris · Damasio et al. 1994, Science (The return of Phineas Gage)
CONTESTEDBlindsight / covert consciousness

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Blindsight: Weiskrantz et al. 1974 (patient D.B.) and the long-studied patient G.Y., who after V1/striate destruction report no conscious vision in the affected hemifield yet perform 2-alternative forced-choice localization, motion and orientation discrimination well above chance. Covert consciousness: Owen et al. 2006 Science - a behaviorally vegetative patient generated reliable, task-specific premotor (tennis imagery) and parahippocampal (spatial navigation) fMRI activation on command, indistinguishable from healthy controls; extended by Monti et al. 2010 NEJM.

ตัวเลขBlindsight forced-choice accuracy typically 70-90% (chance 50%) despite denial of seeing. Owen 2006: n=1, imagery activation sustained ~30 s epochs matching controls. Monti 2010: 54 patients scanned, 5 could wilfully modulate brain activity, 1 answered yes/no autobiographical questions via imagery. ~15-20% of behaviorally unresponsive patients show covert command-following (cognitive-motor dissociation).

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Blindsight: interpretation contested - Phillips (2021) and Overgaard argue it is degraded/low-confidence conscious vision, not truly unconscious, an artifact of conservative subjective criteria vs liberal objective ones (criterion-shift, signal-detection confound), not a dissociation of consciousness from function. Covert-consciousness fMRI single-subject inference is base-rate/false-positive vulnerable; both show mind tracks residual cortex, so remain interpretively neutral on generation vs dependence.

Weiskrantz et al. 1974, Brain · Owen et al. 2006, Science (Detecting Awareness in the Vegetative State) · Phillips 2021, Psychological Review (Blindsight is qualitatively degraded conscious vision)
ESTABLISHEDProcessing without awareness (anesthesia)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Meta-analytic evidence that intraoperative auditory stimuli are processed and can form implicit (unconscious) memory under clinically adequate general anesthesia: primary auditory cortex remains reactive, and priming/behavioral effects appear without explicit recall. Sanders et al. work on 'connected consciousness' via isolated-forearm technique shows a subset of patients respond to command intraoperatively despite later amnesia.

ตัวเลขAuditory-perception meta-analysis (Kratzer/Linassi et al. 2021, Can J Anesth): pooled review; implicit-memory meta-analysis (2021, Life/MDPI): 61 studies, 3,906 patients, implicit-memory effects in 43/119 cohorts (~36%); effect sizes small and heterogeneous. Isolated-forearm studies: ~4-5% of surgical patients show intraoperative connected consciousness.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mundane mechanism + weak/inconsistent effects: 'processing without awareness' reflects preserved low-level sensory cortex and depth-of-anesthesia variability, not a mind operating independent of brain. Implicit-memory effect sizes are small, publication-biased, and inconsistently replicated; positive isolated-forearm responses arguably indicate transient AWARENESS, not unconscious cognition. Fully consistent with brain-dependence.

Linassi/Kratzer et al. 2021, Canadian Journal of Anesthesia (Perception of auditory stimuli during general anesthesia: systematic review & meta-analysis) · Deeprose & Andrade 2006, meta-analytic review of implicit memory under anaesthesia · Sanders et al. 2012, Anesthesiology (Unresponsiveness =/= unconsciousness)
OPENSavant / acquired savant

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Acquired savant cases where prodigious skill emerges after CNS insult: Orlando Serrell (calendar calculation after a baseball head-strike at age 10); Jason Padgett (mathematical/geometric synesthesia after a 2002 assault, later fMRI-documented by Brogaard showing atypical left-hemisphere parietal activation); Derek Amato (sudden musical ability after concussion). Congenital savants (e.g., Kim Peek, hyperlexia/calendar calculation) anchor the phenomenon. Treffert curated the largest case registry.

ตัวเลขAcquired savant syndrome is rare - Treffert catalogued only ~30 acquired cases against a few hundred total savants worldwide as of ~2010s. All are single-case, retrospective reports; no prospective cohort or controlled skill-quantification.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Retrospective single-case anecdote with no pre-injury baseline: skill quantification is post hoc, selection/publicity-biased, and mechanism (release/rewiring vs unmasking of latent capacity) is unfalsified. Even taken at face value, acquired savantism shows brain change ALTERS mind - textbook brain-dependence - and gives no support to any mind-independent reading.

Treffert 2009, Phil Trans R Soc B (The savant syndrome: an extraordinary condition) · Treffert 2014, Scientific American (Accidental Genius / acquired savant) · Brogaard et al. 2013, Neurocase (Jason Padgett acquired synesthesia/savantism)
CONTESTEDPhantom limb

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)The phantom-limb phenomenon itself is robustly established: ~60-80% of amputees experience a vivid felt limb, often painful, correlating with somatosensory cortical reorganization (Flor et al. 1995 Nature: phantom pain magnitude scaled with cortical remapping). Ramachandran & Rogers-Ramachandran 1996 introduced the mirror box, and Chan et al. 2007 NEJM ran the first RCT: mirror therapy reduced phantom pain vs covered-mirror and mental-imagery controls.

ตัวเลขPrevalence of phantom sensation ~80%, phantom pain ~60-70% of amputees. Ramachandran 1996: ~60% reported induced phantom movement. Chan 2007 NEJM: n=22 lower-limb amputees, cross-over; 100% of mirror group improved vs 17% covered-mirror and 33% imagery; controls worsening reversed on crossover to mirror. Flor 1995: cortical reorganization up to ~2-3 cm, strong correlation with pain.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mirror-therapy efficacy is contested: a 2023 systematic review of placebo/sham-controlled RCTs found no convincing evidence of efficacy; trials are small, unblindable, and heterogeneous, so Chan 2007's dramatic result is under-replicated. Regardless, the whole phenomenon (phantom + cortical-remapping cure) is a paradigm case of mind mapping onto brain reorganization - it supports brain-dependence, not any anomalous reading.

Ramachandran & Rogers-Ramachandran 1996, Proc R Soc B (Synaesthesia in phantom limbs induced with mirrors) · Chan et al. 2007, New England Journal of Medicine (Mirror therapy for phantom limb pain) · Flor et al. 1995, Nature (Phantom-limb pain as a perceptual correlate of cortical reorganization)
CONTESTEDFree will (Libet)

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Libet et al. 1983: EEG readiness potential (RP) over motor cortex begins ~550 ms before a self-paced finger flex, whereas subjects' reported conscious 'urge' (W-time via Wundt clock) arises only ~200 ms before movement - implying unconscious brain initiation ~350 ms before awareness. Soon et al. 2008 Nature Neuroscience extended this: fMRI decoded left/right choices from prefrontal/parietal cortex up to ~7-10 s before conscious decision.

ตัวเลขLibet 1983: RP onset ~ -550 ms, conscious W ~ -200 ms, gap ~350 ms; ~40 trials/subject, small n. Soon 2008: decoding accuracy only ~60% (chance 50%) up to 7-10 s ahead - barely above chance. Schurger et al. 2012 PNAS reinterpretation, high citation count.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)Mundane mechanism + interpretive collapse: Schurger et al. 2012 showed the RP is an artifact of averaging time-locked to movement onset - a stochastic accumulator crossing threshold on random noise, NOT a decision signal preceding intention. Introspective W-time is unreliable/retrospective, and Soon's ~60% decoding is far too weak to 'determine' a choice. The experiment does not disprove free will; it fails to clear the bar as evidence about volition at all.

Libet et al. 1983, Brain (Time of conscious intention to act) · Schurger et al. 2012, PNAS (An accumulator model for spontaneous neural activity prior to self-initiated movement) · Soon et al. 2008, Nature Neuroscience (Unconscious determinants of free decisions)

F · The irreducibly open

OPENHard problem of consciousness

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)Chalmers (1995), 'Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness' (J. Consciousness Studies 2:200-219), formulates the explanatory gap: even a complete functional/neural account of discrimination, integration and report leaves unexplained why any of it is accompanied by subjective experience ('why is there something it is like?'). The strongest EMPIRICAL grounding is the COGITATE adversarial collaboration (Cogitate Consortium, Nature 2025, 642:133-142): a preregistered, theory-neutral test of IIT vs Global Neuronal Workspace across fMRI/MEG/iEEG. Even at its most decisive, it adjudicates only the location/dynamics of neural CORRELATES (posterior 'hot zone' sufficiency vs prefrontal necessity) and by design says nothing about why those correlates are experienced at all — an empirical demonstration that the best available science targets the 'easy' problems and leaves the hard problem untouched.

ตัวเลขChalmers 1995: JCS vol 2, pp 200-219; 'explanatory gap' term from Levine 1983. COGITATE: n=256 participants, 3 imaging modalities, preregistered, published Nature 2025 vol 642(8066):133-142. No effect size bears on the hard problem itself — that is the point.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)NOT-EMPIRICAL / interpretive-neutral. The hard problem is a conceptual claim about the limits of physical explanation, not a testable hypothesis; no experiment can confirm or refute it, and the same data (COGITATE) are compatible with physicalist, dualist, panpsychist and illusionist readings. Chalmers's own conclusion (consciousness is non-physical) is contested by illusionists (Dennett, Frankish) who deny the explanandum. It therefore cannot 'clear an evidential bar' because it makes no risky prospective prediction — it is genuinely, structurally open, not merely unresolved.

Chalmers, D. J. (1995). Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness. Journal of Consciousness Studies, 2, 200-219. · Cogitate Consortium et al. (2025). Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information theories of consciousness. Nature, 642, 133-142. · Levine, J. (1983). Materialism and Qualia: The Explanatory Gap. Pacific Philosophical Quarterly, 64, 354-361.
OPEN(revisit) why nothing else stays open

หลักฐานดีสุด (FOR)This is not an empirical phenomenon but a structural/meta claim internal to the CoE framework: that the hard problem is the SOLE irreducibly open item, every other route being either resolved or explicitly closed. The strongest support offered is the framework's own bookkeeping discipline — the §9.3 dead-log (rows L1-L16) that records abandoned mechanisms with grounds, and the §9.4 standing verdict — which shows candidate 'open' problems were adjudicated (Type-A/Type-B test) and either reduced to known physics (Type-B) or logged as cut, rather than left dangling. The empirical backstop is the same COGITATE-style situation: for the 'easy' problems, mundane/neural mechanisms are in principle sufficient, so they do not remain irreducibly open.

ตัวเลขCoE §9.3 dead-log: 16 rows (L1-L16). No effect sizes, sample sizes, p-values apply — the item is definitional, not measured.

critique ชี้ขาด (ทำไมไม่ผ่านบาร์)NOT-EMPIRICAL and self-referential. The claim that 'nothing else stays open' is an artifact of a closed cataloguing system: it is true by the framework's own construction (anything not-yet-closed is provisionally deferred, anything closed is dead-logged), so it is unfalsifiable and cannot be independently replicated. Base-rate/interpretive-neutrality apply: absence of a listed open problem is evidence of curatorial completeness, not of metaphysical closure. It records a decision, not a discovered fact.
