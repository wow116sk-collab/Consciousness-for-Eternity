## What this is

A structured, **working** record of a metaphysical framework developed through extended dialogue and held to strict epistemic discipline. It is **not** a finished scientific theory and does not claim to be. It is best read as a *lens*: an internally consistent, fully attributed synthesis whose empirical, theoretical, and speculative parts are each labelled explicitly, so a reader can see exactly what rests on measurement and what does not.

The single source of truth is [`Consciousness_for_Eternity.md`](./Consciousness_for_Eternity.md). The version is recorded on the document's front line; the full changelog lives in **Appendix A** at the end of that file.

## Epistemic labelling convention

Every major claim in the document carries one of:

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **[ESTABLISHED]** | Confirmed by direct experiment, replicated across labs |
| **[THEORETICAL]** | Mainstream theoretical physics; rigorous but not yet directly tested |
| **[MINORITY VIEW]** | Legitimate but non-consensus position |
| **[CONTESTED]** | Actively disputed within the relevant field |
| **[HYBRID SYNTHESIS]** | Pre-existing components combined in a non-standard configuration |
| **[USER'S OWN]** | Articulated by the author; not found in existing literature |
| **[SPECULATIVE]** | Not empirically supported; included as a parsimonious extension |

Internal audits and objections use a stricter split — **[Fact]** (measured / peer-reviewed), **[Fact-eq]** (derived from a Fact, *with the numbers shown*), and **[Hypo]** (assumption) — so that no challenge enters the load-bearing core unless it is backed by a checkable fact.

## Attribution discipline

Measured/established data is the load-bearing core; hypotheses are flagged as hypotheses; others' ideas are credited by name; adapted ideas are marked as hybrids; the author's name appears only on what he originated. Where the framework's conclusions are independently reached by published researchers (e.g. on mind-uploading identity, or consciousness-as-fundamental), they are cited **for priority, not confirmation** — convergence raises *robustness*, not *truth*, and an unproven position stays **[Hypo]** no matter how many hold it. Each such citation names the single result borrowed and states where it differs, so following a source to its refuted parts does not implicate this framework.
